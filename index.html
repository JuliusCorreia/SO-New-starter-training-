Threat & MITC Concept & levels
MITC mnemonic

Threat is driven by Motivation, Intent and Capability, and is expressed via UK terrorism threat levels.

Threat formula
Threat = Motivation + Intent + Capability
Mnemonic
MITC – “My Intent Creates Threat”
Exam tip
If any element ↑, overall threat can ↑.

Security Behaviour – Golden Rules Insider risk
Think before you share

Every member of staff is part of the security system; behaviour can reduce or create risk.

Rule Why it matters
Be wary of inappropriate questions Social engineering is often used to collect security details
Do not discuss security with those who do not need to know Limits leakage of sensitive procedures
No social media posts about security or procedures Public posts can be used by attackers
Protect PINs, passwords, keys, ID and equipment Compromised access can bypass physical checks; report immediately to Security Manager
Keep uniform branding hidden outside work Reduces impersonation and targeting risks; uniforms must be returned, not discarded
Never misuse ID Access control only works if IDs are used correctly
Consequences: disciplinary action Loss of job Potential criminal charges Serious safety risk
RFX & Prohibited Articles What cannot fly
RFX • SCD (law)

Recognition of Firearms and Explosives (RFX) underpins screening and decision‑making at the checkpoint.

RFX & screening

  • RFX: Recognition of Firearms and Explosives.
  • X‑ray screening is used to detect prohibited articles.
  • Operators must recognise shapes, densities and material signatures.

Prohibited in cabin (examples)

Item Rule
Scissors Blades 6 cm or more from pivot are prohibited
Sharp blades Blade length 6 cm or more prohibited
Razor blades Prohibited as sharp items
Liquids Above allowed volume (follow current regulation for exam context)
Other prohibited articles Any item on the prohibited list for the cabin

Prohibited in cabin & hold

Category Examples
Firearms & ammunition Undeclared firearms, ammunition, gunpowder
Explosives Explosive materials, detonators, fuses
Grenades Fragmentation and smoke grenades
Pyrotechnics Fireworks, party poppers, crackers, similar items

Legal basis: UK Single Consolidated Direction (SCD) sets detailed aviation security requirements and prohibited article rules.

Firearms & Ammunition Definitions & parts

Firearm definition (FA)

  • Any device that allows launching a projectile (solid or liquid).
  • Treated as a firearm if it appears capable of doing so, whether or not it actually works.

Suspicious Signs & Handling On the job
“Stop, step back, call SM”

Suspicious signs of concealment

  • Unusual location of dense items in bags or clothing.
  • Abnormal weight or balance of a bag.
  • Extra stitching or reinforcement.
  • Additional zips not part of the original design.
  • Hidden or extra pockets / compartments.

Handling prohibited items (non‑FA/EX)

Where found Action Search status
Body search Inform Security Manager discreetly Record / report search as incomplete
Bag search Keep item away from passenger, continue search Complete the search, then inform SM; mark as complete

Handling firearms / explosives concealed

Step Action
1 Stop search immediately (body or bag).
2 Discreetly inform the Security Manager.
3 Record / report search as incomplete and follow local SOP.
CT, X‑ray, Colours & Alarms Imaging systems
FISH • EDS • TIP

3D X‑ray / CT – capabilities

Aspect Capability Limitation
Visibility 360° rotation and slice views inside bags Very dense objects reduce clarity
Bag handling See inside without opening; reduces manual checks Items must still fit within tray size
Convenience Liquids and electricals can often stay in bag Cannot handle items larger than the tunnel
Human factor System supports detection and highlighting Subject to operator error and fatigue; limited screen‑time
Other Powerful X‑rays for detailed images Can damage camera film

Mnemonic FISH: Fatigue, Image rotation, Size limit, Heavy (dense) objects.

CT test bag

  • Shaped like a case; used to test calibration and operator response.
  • Shows a combination of a red square and yellow square in the image.
  • Must always be rejected (training / test object, never allowed through).

X‑ray colours & density

Colour Material type Examples
Orange Organic (once living) Food, paper, many explosives
Blue Inorganic (never living) Metals, ceramics, some minerals
Green Mixed (organic + inorganic) Electronics, many homemade explosive mixtures
  • Density: Brighter colour = higher density; lighter colour = lower density.

CIP – Centralised Image Processing

  • Images from different lanes are sent to a central pool.
  • Any trained operator can screen images remotely.
  • Helps balance workload and maintain throughput across lanes.

TIP – Threat Image Projection

  • Injects fake / training images into real X‑ray streams.
  • Used to measure operator performance and alertness.
  • Only one TIP category active at a time: Devices, Firearms, Sharps, Other.
Colour Meaning
Green TIP image correctly detected
Red Incorrect response or time‑out
Yellow Real image – log out and speak to Security Manager
